Who We Are

Sanchaar is a student led initiative based in Jaipur. It was founded to pioneer its young members’ missions to create a more equitable and agreeable world for all. This initiative, thus, aims to promote self-sustenance by skill provision for those with lesser access to resources. In the long run, it hopes to not only empower the economically marginalized individuals economically but also inculcate a desire for learning in the city, and beyond.

Our Vision

Our vision is to see a world where maximum opportunities and resources are being sustainably utilized and that no area, no livelihood, no happiness is lost into oblivion.

Our Mission

The core purpose of the activities undertaken by our initiative would be to realize the potential of the community by enhancing their skills. These activities would not only make them commercially productive but also self sufficient. Furthermore, we plan to integrate creativity, social responsibility and business opportunity to achieve our stated core values.

Our Work

With our goals in mind, Sanchaar has collaborated with various institutes in Jaipur to hold workshops in the recent past.

TIE THE KNOT

A plan that went from a few scribbles on a tissue paper to full-fledged two months long workshop, Tie-The-Knot was a ride that made sure its carriers had all the fun! For this project, Sanchaar collaborated with a well-established NGO in Jaipur, the Jagriti Foundation. Our workshops had over 50 women from in and around rural areas of Jaipur enroll and participate as a place for unleashing their creativity. Sanchaar taught them vocational skills for developing desirable products for the modern market while providing them with the right resources for the same. The products included a range of wrist accessories and traditional rakhis, each with a trendy look to please both the young and old alike. Skills learnt by the women were around the likes of sewing, Aztec braiding, rakhi making etc

#FramingSmiles

#FramingSmiles was a pre-Christmas workshop held at Maansik Vimandit Punarvas Grah, a safe house for differently abled adults. At Sanchaar, we wanted to share the gift of joy and happiness in the spirit of Christmas with these adults. So, we went ahead and held enjoyable, interactive sessions, which began with coloring exercises. The people at Maansik Vimandit Punarvas Grah creatively coloured elephants green, and sun in the shades of blue. We also taught them how to make colourful photo frames. And, after rolling the sheets, and using the glue over cardboard, we ended up framing smiles.

Exhibition:

Sanchaar has participated in Jaipur’s leading exhibitions, like Aspirations and Shubham. It uses these aspirations as a medium to display the products during its workshops, and help the students understand, first hand, the market value of their skill. Once the products are sold at exhibitions, they are either re-invested in Sanchaar’s operations, or handed over to the students.
