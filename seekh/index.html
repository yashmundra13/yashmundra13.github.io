About us

Seekh is an organization that was established in year 2013, with an aim to facilitate and nurture the intellectual growth of the underprivileged children in the rural parts of Rajasthan. Seekh has worked closely with Vatsalya Children’s village which has 60 orphaned children living under the loving care of the Vatsalya community. It has also conducted education programs at Udayan care which is a public charitable trust and works towards empowering the vulnerable children. With the help of few corporate houses, Seekh is trying to enable full year education of underprivileged Children at SOS Children’s Village, Jaipur. Through these endeavours, Seekh aims to fulfil every underprivileged child’s desire to learn.

Vatsalya

Seekh visits a school for orphans named “Vatsalya”, which provides parental care and motherly affection to the destitute children; changing their vagabond life to normal life, in the first week of January. Kids are taught English and Math, through various interactive sessions. This fun filled, yet an informative workshop turns out to be fruitful for the children and the volunteers as well. We believe, at the end of the day, it's not the materialistic pleasure that will bring them satisfaction, but their cognition.

Udayan Care

Udayan is a Sanskrit word meaning 'Eternal Sunshine'. Udayan Care aims to bring sunshine into the lives of underserved sections of society that require intervention. Registered in 1994 as a Public Charitable Trust, Udayan Care works to empower vulnerable children, women and youth, in 14 cities across 9 states of India.
Seekh volunteers have conducted various activities at Udayan care where the children were taught ways to protect the environment by making them do ‘best out of waste’ activities. Seekh plans to continue the visits at this place to nurture these young minds and contribute through any possible means.

SEUP

Seekh’s education for the underprivileged program (SEUP) is a program based on facilitating the education for children who belong to marginalized backgrounds. We’ve partnered with several corporate houses to facilitate this program. Through this program, we’ll strive to enable full year education of as many children as possible at SOS Children’s village, Jaipur. Also, Seekh will take the responsibility of meeting all the needs of these children from procuring their clothes, stationary, organizing field trips etc.
